Inside the 14-day website: how we ship in 1/4 the industry time

Most agencies take 6 weeks because they're running 4 broken processes in parallel. Here's the blueprint-first method that delivers production-ready sites in two weeks.

Rahulkumar· Mar 22, 2026· 6 min read

Most Indian web agencies quote 6-8 weeks for a marketing website. Some quote longer. The reason isn't complexity — it's broken process. Here's exactly how we deliver the same scope in 14 days, and what we do differently.

Why agencies actually take 6 weeks

It's not the work. The actual coding for a 5-page marketing site is 30-50 hours. Six weeks reflects the in-between time:

  • Two weeks of "discovery" gathering inputs that should've been a 45-min call.
  • Three rounds of design mockups before code starts (each round = 1 week of feedback ping-pong).
  • Sequential single-track work (designer → developer → QA, no parallelism).
  • Weekly status meetings instead of asynchronous progress visibility.
  • Multiple project managers between you and the people actually building.

Strip those out, and 6 weeks of calendar time becomes 14 days of build time.

The 14-day blueprint method

Day 1 — strategy call (45 min)

One call, no separate "kickoff", no slide decks. We map your goal, audience, current funnel, and the specific business outcome. Outcome: written notes you can keep.

Day 2-3 — blueprint

A one-page document: scope, page-by-page wireframe descriptions, tech stack, milestones, fixed price. You approve it. No 40-slide proposal. No mockup approval bottleneck. We design directly in code.

Day 4-10 — build

Live staging URL from day one. You see real progress (not Figma mockups) as it happens. WhatsApp updates every 48 hours. Design and code happen together — no handoff delay.

Day 11-13 — refine

Cross-device testing, performance audit (Lighthouse 95+), security review, your feedback round. We don't ship "good enough" — we ship ready.

Day 14+ — launch & support

Production deployment, all credentials handed over, 30 days free post-launch support included.

Why this isn't cutting corners

We deliver the same scope. We just remove the middle layers. Faster doesn't mean less polished — most weeks of agency work are spent waiting for someone to approve something. We replace approval queues with daily visibility.

What you give up to get 14-day delivery

  • No Figma mockups before code (we design in real components on the staging URL).
  • No weekly status meetings (asynchronous WhatsApp updates instead).
  • No project manager middle-layer (you talk to the builder directly).
  • No "phase 1 / phase 2 / phase 3" billing — fixed price, full delivery.

For 90% of marketing websites, you give up nothing you actually wanted.
